The X95M / X95P is a four-band, two-channel parametric equalizer
— a modern interpretation of the classic PEa, W495b, and W495STB modules.

It is not a clone, nor a vague "inspiration."

It is an engineering reconstruction that preserves
the structure and sonic signature of
one of the most recognizable "vinyl-era" equalizers of the 20th century.

The N95 is a careful and respectful transfer
of the character and philosophy of a legend into
a modern context.

For some, the N95 will be a tool.

For others — a part of their studio's sonic identity.

For some — future classic.

· The most critical element is precise channel matching at every point.
This guarantees stereo image integrity.
Only rotary switches are used, and 60-70% of parts are rejected during selection.
· Dual-mono design with two external power supplies significantly reduces noise
— especially during LF boost. As a bonus, spatial imaging is improved.
· A special four-layer PCB topology allows implementation of the fourth band
without increased noise or distortion.
· Through-hole components only.

· The output transformer driver has been modernized
to minimize transformer coloration.
This approach was not available when the PEa / W495 was developed.
· Lundahl output and input mu-core transformers - no comments.
· A total of 34 frequency points — twice as many as the original design.
· Finer gain step resolution — customizable upon request.
· Elma 21 mm knobs, high-contrast front panel graphics, 3U rack
— combining ergonomics, readability, and usability.
